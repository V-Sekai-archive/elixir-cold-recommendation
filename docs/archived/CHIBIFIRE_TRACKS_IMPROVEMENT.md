# chibifire.com: Greatest improvement by creating tracks

How **chibifire.com** can get the largest improvement by defining and investing in **tracks**: focused workstreams (product, community, or capability) that compound over time. This doc scores tracks by *improvement to the org* (capability, brand, sustainability, ecosystem), not only revenue. Companion to [MONETIZATION_TRACKS_SCORED.md](MONETIZATION_TRACKS_SCORED.md) (revenue-focused ranking).

**Product first:** Each track is described as a *path* — rope bridge, busy road, or multilane highway — so the product shape comes before the track name.

---

## Tracks as product (rope bridge, busy road, multilane highway)

Each track is a **product first**: the kind of path you're building determines who can cross and how much traffic you can carry.

| Style | Meaning | When to choose |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Rope bridge** | Narrow, pioneering, one-at-a-time. High differentiation, niche audience, first-mover. |
| **Busy road** | Shared, established, moderate flow. Many users, proven demand, some congestion. |
| **Multilane highway** | High capacity, scalable, infrastructure. Many lanes, high throughput, standards-based. |

**A. Multilane highway → Recsys-in-Elixir product (API + library)**  
Product first: A **multilane highway** — high-capacity infrastructure that many apps plug into. Lanes = API + library + optional managed service; traffic = recommendation requests. Track: one spine (recommendation API + RecGPT/Elixir library); you own the standard and the throughput.

**B. Rope bridge → Multimodal / avatar-asset (vision + 3D, VRChat/Booth-style)**  
Product first: A **rope bridge** — narrow, distinctive, one vertical at a time. First movers (avatar/asset marketplaces) cross; later you reinforce or add lanes. Track: vision + 3D, "recommend by image"; product is the bridge, not the highway.

**C. Busy road → Gaming & storefront (Steam-like, in-game, UGC)**  
Product first: A **busy road** — well-traveled, many vehicles (games, launchers, UGC storefronts). You own the recommendation layer everyone drives on. Track: Steam-like next-game, in-game items; scale by adding partners and lanes.

**D. Multilane highway → Open-source / Hex library (RecGPT as platform)**  
Product first: A **multilane highway** — the open, versioned platform others build on. Lanes = Hex package, docs, API contracts. Track: RecGPT as public spine; product is the road; sustainability from what gets built on top.

**E. Busy road → Consulting & implementation (custom pipelines, pretrain, deploy)**  
Product first: A **busy road** — you help each client build or join the road. Traffic is project-based; the road is shared knowledge and repeatable delivery. Track: custom pipelines, pretrain, deploy; leverage grows when you productize repeat patterns.

**F. Rope bridge → Prediction / trading-adjacent (Figgie, Polymarket-style)**  
Product first: A **rope bridge** — narrow, regulated, high-stakes. Few cross at a time; you own the only bridge for "recsys + prediction/trading" in this terrain. Track: Figgie, Polymarket-style; sustainability high per user, audience limited until you prove the crossing.

**G. Busy road → Low-latency / edge (50ms, real-time, ad-tech)**  
Product first: A **busy road** — time-critical traffic (real-time, ad-tech, sub-50ms). The product is the road's speed and reliability. Track: 50ms target, ad-tech and edge; product is "the fast road"; niche until you have reference deployments.

**H. Busy road → Education (courses, workshops, tutorials)**  
Product first: A **busy road** — many learners cross; you own the curriculum (RecGPT/Elixir recsys, multimodal, pipelines). Track: courses, workshops, or tutorials that teach the stack; product is the learning path and materials.

**I. Busy road → Commissions (affiliate, rev-share, referral)**  
Product first: A **busy road** — revenue when your recommendations drive conversions (sales, signups, trades). Track: commission or rev-share from marketplaces, games, or platforms that use your recs; product is the referral/affiliate layer.

## What “improvement” means here

Improvement = positive change for chibifire as an organization:


| Dimension               | What improves                                                                     |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Capability**          | Skills, tech, and product you can reuse (e.g. multimodal, low-latency, Elixir/ML) |
| **Brand & positioning** | Recognizable niche (e.g. “recsys in Elixir,” “VRChat/avatar recs”)                |
| **Sustainability**      | Revenue or resource that lets the org keep going and invest in more tracks        |
| **Ecosystem**           | Contributors, users, partners, or dependents (Hex, OSS, integrations)             |
| **Leverage**            | One track makes the next track easier (e.g. API → consulting, library → SaaS)     |


Tracks are **concrete workstreams** chibifire can create (own, sponsor, or co-lead) and run for 6–18 months to maximize improvement on these dimensions.

## How to use this for greatest improvement

1. **Create one primary track** that gives the biggest improvement: **Recsys-in-Elixir product** (A). Define it explicitly (e.g. “chibifire Rec API + RecGPT library”), ship a minimal API and/or Hex package, and treat it as the spine for brand, capability, and sustainability.
2. **Create a second track** for differentiation and brand: either **Multimodal / avatar-asset** (B) or **Open-source / Hex library** (D). D amplifies A and ecosystem; B sharpens positioning in a specific vertical.
3. **Add a third track** when the first two are moving: **Gaming & storefront** (C) for reach and reuse of the same stack, or **Consulting** (E) to convert demand into revenue and feedback.
4. **Defer** prediction/trading (F) and low-latency/edge (G) until the primary track is stable, unless you have a clear partner or customer for them.
